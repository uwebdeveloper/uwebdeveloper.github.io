Become an ASI Accredited SUP School

ASI accredited stand up paddle schools have demonstrated they meet the ASI worldwide standards for operations, training and safety.

You also get access to a range of resources, such as procedure manuals and forms specially developed to help with your stand up paddle school business. You can also provide your students with the specially developed ASI stand up paddle courses, complete with learning resources. ASI sets the worldwide standard for accreditation of stand up paddle schools.

Criteria to Become an ASI Accredited SUP School

  • Abide by ASI worldwide Stand Up Paddle Policy and Procedures
  • Employ ASI accredited stand up paddle instructors
  • Have high standards of operational safety and risk management
  • Implement "child protection" and "child friendly" practices
  • Comply with government laws and licences
  • Have appropriate liability insurance cover.   (see insurance page for country liability requirements)
  • Provide quality and safe equipment and clothing (boards, rash vests, wetsuits)
  • Use leashes at all times
  • Prohibit discrimination towards any group or individuals
  • Implement high standards of ethics
  • Have a complaints and feedback process
  • Undergo annual audits to ensure high quality operations and training.
  • Abide by recommended student to instructor ratios:
    • Enclosed flat water locations: (inland waterways .e.g creeks, riveres, lagoons, estuaries, not more than 1 km wide). 8 students to 1 instructor.
    • Exposed Waters: (Coastal beaches & bays & harbours, swell size up to 2 ft, shorebreak no more than 1.5 ft). 6 students to 1 instructor .
    • Surf (non busy) location: (Wave size less than 1½ feet. and no other water users within a 100 metre radius of lesson). 2 students to 1 instructor.
    • Surf (busy) location: (Wave size less than 1½ feet). 1 student to 1 instructor. Abide by recom mended student to instructor ratios
